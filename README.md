# Sri-Lanka-Food-Price-Crisis-Analysis-2004-2025-
🚨 Sri Lanka Food Price Crisis Analysis (2004–2025)

A data-driven investigation of how Sri Lanka’s 2021–2022 economic crisis reshaped food affordability, inflation, and regional inequality.

📊 33,000+ records | 20+ years | 44 markets | End-to-end analytics pipeline

🌍 Project Impact Summary

Sri Lanka’s economic crisis triggered one of the sharpest inflation shocks in its history.

This project quantifies that impact using real-world market data, revealing:

📈 How fast food inflation accelerated during the crisis
🥦 Which essential commodities became most unaffordable
🌍 Which regions were hit the hardest
⚠️ How price stability collapsed after 2020
📊 Whether recovery patterns emerged post-crisis

🎯 Goal: Transform raw food price data into actionable economic insights using SQL, Python, and Tableau.

📸 Dashboard Preview
🔥 Interactive Crisis Impact Dashboard

🧠 Built in Tableau to visualize 20 years of inflation trends, commodity shocks, and regional disparities.

🧩 Key Insights (Executive Summary)
💥 1. Structural Price Shock (2021–2022)

Food prices did not just increase—they accelerated non-linearly during the crisis period, indicating systemic economic stress.

📈 2. Staple Commodities Were Most Vulnerable

Essential foods (rice, lentils, vegetables) experienced significantly higher inflation than processed goods.

🌍 3. Regional Inequality Became More Pronounced

Certain provinces experienced disproportionately higher food inflation, highlighting uneven economic resilience.

⚠️ 4. Market Volatility Increased Sharply Post-2020

Price fluctuations became more frequent and unpredictable, signaling supply chain instability.
